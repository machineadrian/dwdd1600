Artists and Genres

The interesting philoshopical and legal implications of sample-based music should not detract from the genuine beauty created as a result of it. Below are a list of lauded albums and artists created atleast in part, often totally, with samples.

The genres of Hip-Hop, House, French House, Electronica, and Jungle are a few examples of genres owing heavily to musical sampling.

Artists + Works

Note: The lines of genre are not rigid. Many artists, especially those on this list, push and pull to create new and interesting sounds. Genre is not a gospel, but rather, a way for us to broadly define works. Often, works within genre, upon close inspection, will have more in difference than they do in similarity. In sample based music, the lines of genre are additionally blurred as they make use of pre-existing works. The first artist on the list, Kanye West, is famous for using Soul Samples from the 1970s. Is his music Soul? No, however, it may take some concepts from soul. That being said, here are a few artists and genres that make use of sampling.

Hip Hop

The production of instrumental Hip-Hop includes playing samples and "scratching," using record players and the creating a rhythmic backing track, often using a drum machine or sequencer.

  • Kanye West
  • J Dilla
  • Madlib
  • DJ Shadow

Dance

The production of dance music often includes sampled repetitive musical phrase, creating interest through groove, use of EQ, and "4 on the floor" drum patterns.

  • The Avalanches
  • Daft Punk
  • Justice

Jungle

The production of jungle involves speeding up drum breaks, usually sourced from soul or funk records, in combination with vocal samples and melodic loops.

  • Todd Edwards
  • Machinegirl
  • Aphex Twin

Vocabulary

Plunderphonics
As defined by composer John Oswald.
Sampling
Refers to the practice of taking a segment or portion (sample) of one sound recording and incorporating it into another musical composition or recording.
Mashup
A composition technique involving combining 2 unique elements to create a new, dynamic people of music.

History

Included below is an overview of some importance musical moments involving sampling.

  1. 1967 The Tornados' "Globetrotter"
    One of the earliest known instances of sampling. The group took an excerpt of Tchaikovsky's "Swan Lake" and repurposed it for their own composition.
  2. 1988 Public Enemy's "Fight The Power"
    Makes use of a James Brown drum sample, Funky Drummer. Today, James Brown is one of the most sampled artists in Hip-Hop.
  3. 2000 The Avalanches' "Since I Left You"
    A seminal dance album made completely of samples, some of which are still unknown to this day.
  4. 2004 Kanye West's "Through The Wire"
    Early breakthrough for a style known as "chipmunk-soul," a technique involving speeding up soul songs for use in Hip-Hop. Chaka Khan, the artist sampled in this track, has said about it: "“I was upset about sounding like a chipmunk,”
